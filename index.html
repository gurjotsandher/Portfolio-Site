Who I am

aspiring dev | academic | learner

My name is Gurjot Sandher I'm currently enrolled at BCIT in the Technical Programming specialty based on Microsoft technologies, such as C#, Azure, and more.

Prior to BCIT I was at the University of Pecs Medical School. It was a really great experience that developed my mind and gave me experiences that shaped who I am today. I gained acceptance to their medical program when I was 19 years old. To this day it was one of the best moments of my life.

Unfortunately due to personal and family problems I had to return home to Vancouver. I became the sold caretaker for my teenage brother and no longer had the means to spend an additional 4 years in school and go through residency. During this time I decided to try and learn how to code and never looked back since.

My 'Hello World' moment came as I learnt about the internet of things. As a 90s kid I saw the internet blow up as a grew up. From the days of LimeWire to now Spotify as a child I always wondered what were computers. I first got access to the internet when I was in the 4th grade at the public library. I'd get 45 minutes per a week and I would run there after school. By the time I was in middle school I was all about AOL, MSN, and you name it. When I got my own PC I realized I had a whole world in just my very own bedroom.

My 'Hello Universe' moment came after my first year at BCIT. I had the foundation to begin learning anything I desired. My first course of action was to start building my own applications. Along with my team we began diving into the world of FaceBook's React and Amazon's AWS technologies to build our first hosted application. At this time I found I now had the means to create powerful things with the new concepts and technologies that I had learnt.

My goal for 2021 is to shift from my Java background to C# and continue building on the skills I have laid foundations in. I'm hoping to find a mentor and internship opportunity to catalyze my development. I've beaten the odds in my life and I plan to continue thriving wherever my programming journey may take me. If you take a chance on me I guarantee I will not let you down.
